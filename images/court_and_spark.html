Product description

Product Description

Most everybody's favorite Joni Mitchell album, highlighted by Help Me; Same Situation; Raised on Robbery ; and Free Man in Paris.

Amazon.co.uk

Painter-turned-folksinger Joni Mitchell had slipped stark saxophone solos into her prior album, For the Roses, and her singing had often hinted at a capacity for bluesier fare than her guitar- and piano-framed confessional ballads offered. None of those hints prepared fans for this sudden, expansive shift towards a much larger canvas--a sleeker, orchestrated pop style pulsating with jazz elements. Court & Spark found Mitchell casting aside her earth mother affectations and revealing herself as the thoroughly modern, thoroughly complicated woman she is; the songs sustained familiar preoccupations with relationships but replaced courtly settings and naturalistic imagery with recognisably modern locales. Deeply romantic, constantly questioning, classic tracks like the title song, "Help Me", "Free Man in Paris", "Same Situation" and "Raised on Robbery" display a more liberated Mitchell, ready to rumble with unbridled electric guitars (guest Robbie Robertson on "...Robbery"), even willing to poke fun at her own oh-so-sensitive rep with a hip cover of Annie Ross's hilarious "Twisted". --Sam Sutherland

5.0 out of 5 stars Refined Mitchell
Reviewed in the United Kingdom on 22 June 2018
Verified Purchase
"Court and Spark" from 1974 is one of Mitchell's most successful albums; both artistically and commercially. She continues her move away from the folk genre that she started from and she is here very wide in her musical expression. A jazzy and slightly funky approach is quite predominant.
The album contains one of Mitchell's biggest single hits with the almost Steely Danish "Help Me". A great and refined recording. Also the almost pure rock'n roll "Raised on Robbery" was a minor hit.
Other fine songs include the title track, which might lead the mind to Mitchell's English counterpart in the 1970s; Sandy Denny.
Among the more jazzy songs are my favorites "Car on a Hill", "Just Like This Train" and "Trouble Child".
Classic Mitchell is "Free Man in Paris", where she vocally is supported by David Crosby and Graham Nash.
The end song "Twisted" sounds like something that might have suited Georgie Fame perfectly

I have given both these CDs a 5 star rating as I am a huge fan of Joni Mitchell and, in my opinion, they epitomise all that is brilliant and beautiful about her music. It's hard to believe these were recorded back in the 1970s as they sound as fresh and original as ever. Most of the tracks are very strong and are still relevant today. Joni is an excellent and talented guitarist and plays many instruments on her albums including the piano. My favourite song on Court and Spark is Free Man in Paris and on Ladies of the Canyon it's a choice between Conversation and Priest. In conclusion, I would highly recommend both of these CDs to anyone who appreciates well written music with amazing lyrics.

5.0 out of 5 stars Another 1970's classic album
Reviewed in the United Kingdom on 26 April 2012
Verified Purchase
If you like Joni Mitchell you will love this and if you don't like her this might be a good place to start because Court and Spark is probably one of Joni's more commercial offerings and so may have a wider appeal. While Court and Spark, Help me, Free Man in Paris and the rocking Raised on Robbery are the more commercial tracks; People's Parties, Same Situation, Car on the Hill and Down to You are more of the analysis of relationships for which Joni is famous/infamous. Add the amusing Twisted as a final track and this shows Joni's multi talented approach to music. As ever intelligent lyrics and support from a who's who of artists including David Crosby, Graham Nash, Jose Feliciano, Joe Sample, Wilton Felder, Robbie Robertson, and Larry Carlton mean that it sounds great. The version that I purchased has not been remastered but it still sounds excellent to me, as did the original vinyl I had back in the 70's.
